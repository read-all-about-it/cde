Developing and maintaining the *To Be Continued: The Australian Newspaper Fiction Database* is a massive undertaking, and involves people across multiple universities and cultural institutions.

It would not be possible without the many dedicated librarians, archivists and digitisers who have built the National Library of Australia's Trove database. The people listed below are those who have helped put the database together and are continuing to grow it. But the *To Be Continued* community is much bigger and grows every day. Anyone who is interested in the fiction and newspaper cultures of 19th and 20th centuries can get involved --- find out more.

## Dr Katherine Bode

Kath is a Professor of Literary and Textual Studies at the Australian National University in Canberra. Her research explores literary phenomena with computational methods and computational phenomena with literary methods. Along with Carol, she is editor of the *To Be Continued* Database.

## Carol Hetherington

Carol is a librarian, literary scholar and bibliographer. She has worked with academic journal *Australian Literary Studies* as a bibliographer and researcher for many years and as Content Manager of the AustLit database (2001--2012).

## Dr Roger Osborne

Roger is Associate Professor of English Literature at James Cook University in Cairns. His research looks at Australian literature and British Modernism as seen through the lens of book history, print culture and scholarly editing. This research includes the study of authorship, editing, reading and publishing in Australian and international periodicals.

## Galen Cuthbertson

Galen is a PhD candidate at the Australian National University who researches the application of large language models and complex machine learning to literary reception studies. He has been the Developer for the *To Be Continued Database* since 2020, and is responsible for developing its infrastructure and for building its forthcoming Collaborative Digital Editing platform.

## Junran Lei

Junran is Development Officer in the Centre for Digital Humanities Research at the Australian National University. She worked as researcher to investigate the development of an open source archival repository and preservation system for UNESCO and is currently working on OCCAMS, an online cultural collection analysis. She was instrumental in setting up the *To Be Continued* Database in 2018.

## Fiannuala Morgan

Fiannuala is a PhD candidate at the Australian National University with an interest in geospatial analysis of 19th century 'Australian' literature. Her research explores the use of quantitative and computational methods in the humanities and, in particular, the use of digital mapping software. She has been working with the To Be Continued Database since 2019 to explore bushfires in colonial fiction.

## Neil Hogan

Neil is a PhD student at the Australian National University in Canberra. He is researching Australian space opera and other speculative fiction in early 20^th Century newspapers. He joined the *To Be Continued* team in 2023 to help recover lost fiction and make it available again for future generations.

## Elizabeth Smyth

Elizabeth is a researcher and librarian at James Cook University's Nguma-bada campus. Her research involves writing a contemporary magic realist novel that explores farming and ambition through human relationships with nonhuman nature. She joined the *To Be Continued* project in 2023 to apply a georgic work ethic to scrubbing data.

## Clare Burnett

Clare is an award-winning journalist and a PhD candidate at Griffith University in Brisbane. Her research explores the development of literary cultures and the proliferation of international fiction in Australian newspapers with a focus on the Gothic. She joined the *To Be Continued* team in 2023 to identify and collate more Gothic literature with which to startle and scare.
